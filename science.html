2. Try to Attempt Full Paper

3. Be Careful While Solving Numericals

Stay careful with Numericals as even sign can be responsible for losing marks or the whole sum. Use proper values and signs especially of (g). Underline the units while reading question. Write in detail about each step and formula that you are using and give proper Conclusion.

B. Preperation Tips


1. Try to Write Answers by your by Own

Learn concepts and try to write it by your own in own words. This will help to make answers by own concepts and can be really helpful in subject like Science.


2. Read Masterkey and Textbook

Read mastery and Textbook to understand indepth concepts. Underline main points and learn each table in Textbook and Masterkey. Learn the Textbook and Masterkey in such way that there is power in each Lesson to create 100 MCQ's. If you have to score then you have to do it.


3. Learn Concepts not Answers

Learn each and every concept from the Textbook. Don't learn answer, try to learn concept behind it. Remmember that only 10% of the Science boards paper is made on textual questions; other 90% is based on concepts. So try to learn concepts by making notes by your own with help of Textbook and other Resource.


4. See One shot Lectures for every Chapter

See One shot lectures for every Chapter on youtube as each concept is thought to us in detail. Along with that make self notes. My personal favourite youtuber was Prath Momaya for Science.


5. Revision

In studying, revision will help you remember the important facts, figures, topics and methodologies that you studied in the past. In examinations, revision will help you be better able to answer test questions. You'll feel prepared.


6. Sample Paper Practice

Using Sample papers, students will witness a boost in confidence with regular practice, answering questions that they can answer and realising how well-prepared they are for the test. The exam Sample paper can make students prepare for board exams or any regular test convenient to appear.



C. How to Study?

1. Complete your syllabus in detail

Learn all the Textual answers and objectives from Textbook / Masterkey as most of paper is based on Textual Questions.


2. Frequent revision

Social Science is a theoretical subject and requires time and proper break instead of practice. It is better to revise topics regularly. This will help in relieving the stress and relaxation for you next study session.


3. Take class text, UT, half yearly, Preboard exams seriously.

Study for every test and don't avoid it. Tests helps us to revise full syllabus and study which is as important as Sample paper practice.


4. Practice Sample papers /PYQ's

Using sample papers, you will witness a boost in confidence. With regular practice, answering questions you can answer and realise how well-prepared you are for the test. The exam sample paper can make you well-prepared for board exams or any regular test convenient to appear.
