Social Science






A. Tips to Write Social Science Paper


1. Presentation is important

Maintain an appropriate pace and handwriting throughout the paper. A good Presentation will certainly cast a good impression on the Moderator. You may underline the bullet points you wish to make – this saves a lot of Moderator's time. Attempt all the questions in a proper sequence- section a, section b and section c.This will help alot.


2. Try to Attempt Full Paper

Try to Attempt full paper with options. This will help to be on safe side.


3. Highlight Keywords

You must underline the bullet points. This saves a lot of Moderator's time. Highlight the important points like main answers.


4. Avoid Spelling Mistakes

If your answer is very good but it contains spelling mistakes then it can spoil your whole answer. If you don't know spelling of any of the word then use similar word of it but avoid spelling mistakes. Thus practice it and try to avoid it.


5. Use Rough Paper to Write Ideas

Before writing any answer write down ideas on rough paper and then start writing answers. This will help to improve writing Accuracy


6.Rechecking is Must

Re-check the question paper for unattempt questions and make corrections, if required. Reserve the last 15 minutes for Revision.



B. Preperation Tips


1. Be familiar with the theory

Social Science is a theoretical subject. Hence, theory plays an important role. Before starting the preparation for your exams, make sure to read the chapters first. You do not need to know the chapters in detail but a brief knowledge of all the chapters will always be helpful.


2. Focus on MCQ's and Find out wrong pair

As sometimes these don't come from textual objectives. So in order to secure few marks, read full textbook and masterkey in detail. Highlight main points that you think will come in exams and do revision of it systematically.


3. Do not Ignore Maps and Graphs

Maps and Graphs are ignored by many students. If there’s one question that you could be sure to show up in your exam it would be either maps or Graphs. Go through all the maps and Graphs, and practice them at least thrice.


4. Read Non-Textual Questions from set 21

Sometimes boards even ask non-textual answer. In order to be prepared for this read many non-textual answers from set 21.


5. Learn all Pictures of Temples, Ancient monuments, Paintings

Remember all the pictures of temples, places, painting from the textbook and try to elaborate them. Sometimes this question comes for 3 marks


6. Previous years question papers are substantial

Once you are done with chapter-test and sample papers, you must solve previous years’ question papers. These question papers provide you the same experience of giving a board examination. Whenever you solve past years’ question papers always time yourself. Give yourself a 3- hour deadline and try to complete the paper within the given deadline. Once you are done with it either check it yourself or ask someone else to do it. This would help you analyze your entire preparation.



C. How to Study?


1. Complete your syllabus in detail

Learn all the Textual answers and objectives from Textbook / Masterkey as most of paper is based on Textual Questions.


2. Frequent revision

Social Science is a theoretical subject and requires time and proper break instead of practice. It is better to revise topics regularly. This will help in relieving the stress and relaxation for you next study session.


3. Take class text, UT, half yearly, Preboard exams seriously.

Study for every test and don't avoid it. Tests helps us to revise full syllabus and study which is as important as Sample paper practice.


4. Practice Sample papers /PYQ's

Using sample papers, you will witness a boost in confidence. With regular practice, answering questions you can answer and realise how well-prepared you are for the test. The exam sample paper can make you well-prepared for board exams or any regular test convenient to appear.
