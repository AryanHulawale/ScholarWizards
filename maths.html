MATHS






A. Tips to write Maths Paper


1. Explain each step to Examiner

You should keep in mind that the examiner has to understand every step of your answer so we should write answer in such a way that Examiner is a kid and we are Teacher teaching him the answer. Examiner cuts half marks if any step is missing so write each and every step of the answer.


2. Use Brackets for Important Formula/Theorem

If you are using any Formula or Theorem in the answer mention it in the bracket or your marks will be deducted by Moderator.


3.Write Down All the Steps

While solving a problem, your steps are equally important as the end result. Make sure you answer every question neatly by writing all the steps until you arrive at the solution. This will not only help you understand the problem better, but also fetch you more marks for paying attention to the solution.


4. Rechecking is Must

Rechecking should be done as numbers and signs can create Silly mistakes, in order to correct it rechecking is must. Rechecking should be done especially in Geometry. Check whether you have written all the Formulas and Theorem in brackets. This can save your lots of marks.


5. Solve Extra Questions at last

If he have time at last after revision then solve extra questions. This will help you because the answer which have least marks is put in option. Suppose your marks got deducted in one answer but your option wala question got out off marks then previous answer will be considered as option. Thus you can go on Safe side.


6. Avoid Silly Mistakes

Avoid silly mistakes in Maths. Give proper attention to the Signs in algebra. keep your mind calm and then solve question. This will help to avoid silly mistakes.


7. Keep page Neat and Clean

Write answer systematically to look it presentable. Make box with pencil for the final answer. Attempt all answers sequence wise. This will help Moderator and can save his time.



B. Preperation Tips


1. Keep Practicing

The more you practise, the better you will become. Solve as many mathematical problems as you can, and make sure to practise a wide range of problems to cover all the different types of questions that can be asked. Students can use mock test papers provided by different resources. For example, a small booklet deskwork contains around 15 mock papers based on the syllabus of Class 10 maths.
